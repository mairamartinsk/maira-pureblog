To start my piano journey, I opted for Faber Adult Piano Adventures books, rather than the ubiquitous Alfred's. The reason being, Alfred focuses too much on left hand chords and (very ugly and outdated) pop music, and Faber focuses on hand independence and classical music. I know that if I start with classical and sheet music, I'll be able to play anything in the future!

So far, this first month has been HUMBLING. I thought "I'm such a smart girl, for sure I'll get ultra advanced, ultra fast". But no. Turns out, it's much harder than I thought to coordinate both hands, learn to read music, to count beats, to feel the rhythm. *Alas*, I'm a child again.
