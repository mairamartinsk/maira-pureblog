I first learned "The Shepherd Plays" (or "The Shepherd's Flute") a couple months ago, following the progression of the Russian piano method [book](https://www.halleonard.com/product/48010304/the-russian-school-of-piano-playing) we use at [PCA](https://www.pianocareeracademy.com/).

Recently I realised the score was in my Keith Snell Grade 1 repertoire [book](https://kjos.com/essential-piano-repertoire-level-one.html) -- with *pedal* markings! I haven't really learned to use the pedals yet, so I was super excited to give it a try. 😀

<lite-youtube videoid="l68mbtLbci0"></lite-youtube>

Tatiana Salutrinskaya was a Russian/Soviet pianist and composer. Sadly, as is often the case with female composers, not much is known about her life. [This](https://musopus.net/musicians/salutrinskaya-tatiana/) is what I could find on her.
